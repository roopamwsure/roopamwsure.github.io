Suggested Talks

Sovereign AI for Enterprises: Control, Compliance, Infrastructure, and Trust

Sovereign AI is no longer only a national policy topic. For enterprises, it is becoming an operating model for controlling where AI runs, where data and keys reside, how models are governed, how evidence is produced, and how regulated workloads can move from experimentation to trusted production.

Best fit: Government, regulated-industry, cloud, infrastructure, AI governance, enterprise architecture, and digital transformation audiences.

From Data Residency to Sovereign AI: What Enterprise Leaders Need to Govern

Data residency is only one part of sovereignty. This session explains the broader enterprise control model: infrastructure location, operational authority, identity and key management, AI model governance, observability, compliance evidence, and jurisdictional risk.

Best fit: CIO, CTO, CISO, public sector, financial services, healthcare, telecom, and critical infrastructure audiences.

Governing Agentic AI: Identity, Memory, Policy, Provenance, and Human Control

As enterprises move from copilots to autonomous and semi-autonomous agents, governance must extend beyond model-level controls. This session explains how organizations can govern agentic AI across identity, policy, memory, tool use, provenance, observability, drift, and human supervision.

Best fit: AI governance events, enterprise architecture forums, risk and compliance webinars, CIO/CTO audiences, data and AI leadership communities.

From AI Pilots to Governed Intelligence: Why Enterprises Need an AI Governance Layer

Many AI programs succeed in pilots but struggle in production because governance is fragmented across models, retrieval systems, business applications, policy engines, observability tools, and human workflows. This session introduces a vendor-neutral control-plane view for trusted enterprise intelligence.

Best fit: Enterprise AI conferences, responsible AI programs, platform engineering communities, regulated-industry technology leaders.

Why Enterprise AI Fails: The Knowledge-System Problem Behind Model Failure

Not every AI failure is a model failure. Many failures come from stale knowledge, weak metadata, broken permissions, poor retrieval, missing provenance, or ungoverned orchestration. This session introduces governed knowledge systems as the foundation for durable enterprise AI.

Best fit: RAG, data governance, knowledge management, AI platform, and enterprise architecture audiences.

AGEval-1: How to Evaluate Governed Agentic AI Before Production

Enterprises need evaluation methods that test not only model quality, but also authorization behavior, memory use, tool execution, provenance, escalation, drift, and audit evidence. This session outlines an evaluation approach for governed agentic AI systems before production release.

Best fit: AI risk, model governance, AI assurance, enterprise AI operations, and regulated-sector technology programs.
